# Screenshot Tour

The activity overview lets you search and filter your activities and shows totals for the current selection:

![](images/screenshot-overview.png)

Each activity has its own page with an interactive map:

![](images/screenshot-activity-view.png)

… as well as plots for distance, speed, and elevation over time:

![](images/screenshot-activity-time-series-plots.png)

You also get a beautiful interactive heatmap of all your activities:

![](images/screenshot-heatmap.png)

If you're into _explorer tiles_ or _squadratinhos_, this got you covered. Here is the cluster at zoom level 14 …

![](images/screenshot-explorer-14.png)

… and the much finer zoom level 17:

![](images/screenshot-explorer-17.png)

Every activity also shows which explorer tiles you have newly unlocked:

![](images/screenshot-new-tiles.png)

You can define segments along a route and the app matches your activities to them automatically, tracking forward and backward direction separately:

![](images/screenshot-segment.png)

… together with a histogram of how your time on the segment is distributed:

![](images/screenshot-segment-time-histogram.png)

The Eddington number tells you how many days you have exceeded a given distance, and how far you'd need to go to push it up:

![](images/screenshot-eddington-1.png)

… visualized as the point where the cumulative distance curve crosses the diagonal:

![](images/screenshot-eddington-plot.png)

Plenty of summary statistics let you track how many rides, walks, or hikes you have done over the years:

![](images/screenshot-summary-stats.png)

Geotagged photos are matched to the nearest activity and shown right on the map:

![](images/screenshot-photo-map.png)

Share pictures let you export a clean map and key figures of an activity:

![](images/screenshot-share-picture.png)

Uploading new activities is just a matter of dropping a file:

![](images/screenshot-upload.png)

The configuration options are available within the interface such that you do not have to work with configuration files (like in earlier versions):

![](images/screenshot-settings.png)

A light mode is available as well, in case you don't like the dark theme:

![](images/screenshot-settings-light-mode.png)
